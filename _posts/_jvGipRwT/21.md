Hmmm! It seems to mean about the same either way, but it does point to a problem with modals (that take whole predicates as complement), namely that the attachment of an object is always ambiguous in just this way -- though usually context does decide or else it makes no difference. "One brings (causes to come) something to craziness (object of 'kama') " or "one causes that something is crazy (object of vt 'nasa')."
